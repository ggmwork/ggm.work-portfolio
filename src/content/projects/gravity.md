**Gravity Generator — 3D Prop**
*3D Modeling · Texturing · Real-Time Rendering*

---

Gravity Generator was created as part of a job application technical assessment — a brief to design, model, texture, and present a production-ready sci-fi prop that demonstrated both artistic sensibility and technical discipline within a professional pipeline.

The creative direction I set for myself was simple: it had to look engineered, not decorative. Every panel, every seam, every component needed an implied purpose. To get there I went deep into references — industrial machinery, sci-fi props, energy devices — specifically studying how realism is built through material logic rather than geometry complexity. Wear patterns in the right places, roughness variation across surfaces, mechanical segmentation that suggests the thing actually works. The final mesh landed at 9,552 polygons — detailed enough to read as complex, optimized enough to be genuinely game-ready. Textures were developed in Substance Painter at 4K PBR, then brought into Unreal Engine for the final lighting and presentation setup, where the material response under dynamic light is what either proves or breaks that kind of work. *[Images]*

It was a compact, focused exercise — but focused is sometimes exactly what a project needs to be. One asset, done properly, end to end.
