**Reset The Sun — Game Development**
*3D Art · Animation · Environment Design · Trailer Production*

---

Reset The Sun was a seven-month university project built to simulate a real indie production pipeline — a 2.5D narrative puzzle game centered on time manipulation, with the ambition of delivering not just a playable vertical slice but a complete market-ready presentation around it. It was the most cross-disciplinary project I'd worked on up to that point, and my contributions stretched across most of it: character modeling and animation, environment art, material systems, trailer production, and parts of the marketing effort.

The game's core mechanic was deliberately minimal — time reversal, one system, executed well. The creative challenge that came from that constraint was making the world do the heavy lifting. With simple mechanics, environmental storytelling had to carry the atmosphere and the mystery. The world needed to feel broken, reactive, like time itself was unreliable. Every asset, every material choice, every lighting decision was in service of that feeling.

My first major contribution was the protagonist. The team debated the visual direction early — stylized or grounded — and we landed on a realistic low-poly approach, a character that felt human enough to identify with but clean enough to animate clearly and read well in a side-scrolling context. I modeled and animated her, with particular attention to the locomotion cycles, which in a puzzle game with deliberate pacing need to feel weighted and intentional rather than generic.

In parallel I developed environment assets and rebuilt the project's material system into consolidated master materials — metals, surfaces, organic textures all derived from shared bases. Beyond the obvious performance gains, it meant visual consistency was structural rather than something we had to police manually.

The piece of work I'm most proud of on this project is the trailer. I wanted a single unbroken sequence — one shot — where environments fracture, collapse, and rebuild across timelines, communicating the mechanic visually without a single line of explanation. Composited in After Effects with Stable Diffusion used for select generative elements, edited and sound-designed in Premiere Pro. The goal was to make someone who'd never heard of the game understand its identity in under a minute.

Seven months, a small team, a lot of hats. Reset The Sun was where I learned what cross-functional ownership actually means in practice — not just contributing to a project, but caring about the whole thing.
