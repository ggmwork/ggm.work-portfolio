**Better Call Saul  Kinetic Typography**
*Motion Design · Compositing · Video Editing*

---

This was a four-week academic project: take a three-minute scene from *Better Call Saul* and reinterpret it entirely through kinetic typography. Not subtitles, not captions  animated text as a visual language that carries the emotional weight of the dialogue on its own.

The first decision that shaped everything else was how the text would exist within the scene. Flat overlays would have been the easy path, but I wanted the typography to feel spatially embedded  text appearing behind a character's shoulder, in front of an object, shifting depth as the camera moves. That required rotoscoping every relevant frame using Rotobrush 2.0 in After Effects, separating subjects from backgrounds to create the layered plates that made it possible. It was the most time-consuming part of the process by far, and it's the decision I'd make again without hesitation  the difference in how the final piece reads is significant.

For the visual language I went back to the show itself: its color palette, its typographic restraint, the way it builds tension through pacing rather than spectacle. The font and color choices were deliberately aligned with the series identity, not imposed on top of it. The typography had to feel like it belonged to that world. Scene segmentation in Premiere Pro gave me precise timing control over every cut, which meant the animation rhythm could track the dialogue's natural tension rather than fighting it.
