Alongside the larger projects, I produced a range of visual assets for NexLed some planned, some urgent, all with their own constraints.

The most technically involved was a 3D product render for NexLed's website and social media. I worked in Blender, with one significant obstacle: the office PC couldn't handle the project at any usable speed, let alone render it. My solution was to use a cloud render farm, which kept the project moving without compromising on quality. The concept I developed was a short sequence opening on a living room scene to give the product context, then pulling focus onto the downlight itself, ending with an exploded view of its components to highlight build quality. I imported the CAD model, built the materials and textures, set up the rigging and camera controls, then brought in a living room scene that I trimmed and simplified to reduce render load. After several rounds of revision, the final video came together. *[Video]* It's also serving as the foundation for a series  I'm currently producing the same treatment for the LED bar.

The garage door graphics came with a different kind of friction. I was asked to replace an existing design that wasn't working, and I flagged that it would be smarter to wait until the rebranding was further along so everything would be consistent. That was overruled it was needed immediately. I designed it anyway, working with what I had at the time and keeping it close enough to the direction the brand was heading.

The vehicle wraps were a format I hadn't worked in before. Two company trucks, specific panels, real-world constraints. I took measurements and photos on site, used Photoshop to mock up the designs in context, and produced multiple variations for each panel so my superior could make informed decisions. Beyond these, I produced a range of supporting visuals throughout the year website slides, document imagery, and product images using Photoshop, Affinity, and AI-assisted generation with manual refinement. The kind of work that doesn't headline a portfolio but keeps everything else looking coherent.
